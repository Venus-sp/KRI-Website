About Us

Kababaihang Rizalista, Inc. (KRI) is a civic, cultural, and non-profit women's organization dedicated to honoring the life, works, and ideals of Dr. José Rizal, the national hero of the Philippines. Inspired by Rizal's teachings on education, patriotism, and social responsibility, the organization aims to promote national consciousness and moral values among its members and the wider community.

As the women's counterpart of the Knights of Rizal, KRI plays an important role in preserving and sharing Filipino heritage and identity. Founded in the late 1950s, Kababaihang Rizalista has grown from a small group of committed women into a nationwide and international organization with active chapters across the Philippines and abroad.

It is composed of women from diverse backgrounds who share a common commitment to civic service, cultural preservation, and community development. The organization is non-sectarian, non-partisan, and inclusive, focusing on unity and service rather than political or religious affiliation. Through various programs such as educational outreach, cultural activities, community service projects, and commemorative events, Kababaihang Rizalista continues to bring Rizal's ideals to life in today's society. The organization empowers women to become leaders and active contributors to nation-building while inspiring younger generations to value education, integrity, and patriotism. At its core, KRI remains committed to serving both the Filipino community and the global society through meaningful action and shared values.

Mission

Our mission is to empower women through education, cultural awareness, and community participation. Inspired by the ideals of Dr. José Rizal, KRI promotes unity, leadership, patriotism, and social responsibility.

Vision

Our Vision is to build a united and empowered community of Filipinos abroad that upholds the ideals of Dr. José Rizal and contributes positively to society, guided by values of respect, integrity, knowledge and compassion.

History & Inspirations

The Kababaihang Rizalista Inc. (KRI) is a women’s organization founded in 1953 by Adelaida Rodriguez in the Philippines. Inspired by the ideals of Dr. José Rizal, the group was established to promote the vital role of women in nation-building, as emphasized in his Letter to the Young Women of Malolos. KRI is dedicated to fostering education, cultural awareness, civic responsibility, and moral values among women. Through various programs and community initiatives, it continues to uphold Rizal’s vision of empowered, educated, and socially responsible Filipinas.

Growth Beyond the Philippines

As Filipinos migrated across the globe, Kababaihang Rizalista expanded internationally. Chapters were established across Asia, the Middle East, North America, and Europe, serving as cultural and social support networks for Filipina women abroad.

Kababaihang Rizalista in Germany

In Germany, Kababaihang Rizalista chapters were established in key cities such as Frankfurt, Munich, Stuttgart, and other areas with active Filipino communities. These chapters were formed by Filipina migrants and professionals seeking unity, cultural preservation, and empowerment. The organization in Germany promotes Filipino values and Rizal’s teachings through cultural events, community outreach, and charitable activities. It also plays an important role in supporting Filipinas as they integrate into German society while maintaining a strong connection to their Filipino heritage. Today, KRI in Germany continues to strengthen the Filipino community by fostering solidarity, encouraging leadership among women, and preserving the rich cultural identity of the Philippines abroad.

Inspiration from Dr. José Rizal

Dr.José Rizal is the national hero of the Philippines. He used his writings to inspire reform and national awareness.

While living in Europe, including Berlin, Germany, he completed parts of his famous novel Noli Me Tangere, which revealed social injustices. His ideas continue to inspire Kababaihang Rizalista in promoting education, empowerment, and cultural pride.
